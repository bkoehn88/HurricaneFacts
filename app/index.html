Hurricane Katrina

Hurricane Katrina (2005) was the costliest natural disaster to ever hit the United States. Property damages amounted to an estimated $108 billion, and the death toll was at least 1,833 according to the U.S. National Hurricane Center.

Hurricane Camile

Hurricane Camille (1969) had the strongest winds of any U.S. landfalling hurricane. It was also a costly one, costing the U.S. 259 lives and $1.42 billion according to the National Hurricane Center.

1900 Galveston

Hurricane

The Great Galveston Hurricane of 1900 resulted in the most deaths of any U.S. natural disaster, totalling approximately 8,000 deaths.

1935 Labor Day

Hurricane

The labor day hurricane of 1935 had the highest intensity and strongest winds of any hurricane to hit the U.S. It took approx. 500 lives and cost the U.S. around $6 million.
